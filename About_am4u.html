AM4U (Apparel Made for You) / Critical Mass is the company that I have worked for for about eight months.
Here is a basic overview of how the process of creating a product works at the company:

  1. A purchase order is made by a customer (e.g. designer company)
  2. Software rearranges pieces of clothing to take up as much space as possible on given paper or fabric (minimizes wasted paper & fabric)
  3. Designer's pieces are printed on inkjet printer(s) that prints up to 60 incehs wide
  4. Paper with designer's pieces are transferred through heat press machine with fabric held on top of it
  5. Paper and fabric come out of the end of the heat press with design transfered to fabric
  6. Roll of fabric with designer's pieces are automatically cut on cutter
  7. Individual pieces are taken, organized, and labeled to be sewn either in the company or for another company to sew
  8. After the pieces are sewn together, the designer's cloth(es) has/have been created (this can be shirts, pants, scarves, etc.)
